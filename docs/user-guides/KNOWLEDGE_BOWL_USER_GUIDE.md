# Knowledge Bowl Training Guide

**For UnaMentis Users**

## Welcome to Knowledge Bowl Training!

Knowledge Bowl is an academic team competition popular in Colorado, Minnesota, and Washington. UnaMentis helps you prepare for competitions with realistic training modes that simulate actual match conditions.

## Getting Started

### Choosing Your Region

Knowledge Bowl rules vary by state. When you first open Knowledge Bowl in UnaMentis:

1. Go to **Knowledge Bowl** from the main menu
2. Tap **Settings** (gear icon)
3. Select your region: **Colorado**, **Minnesota**, or **Washington**
4. Rules and scoring automatically adjust to your region

**Why this matters:** Each region has different rules for conferring (team discussion), scoring, and match format. Training with the right rules prepares you for your actual competitions.

### Understanding the Dashboard

The Knowledge Bowl dashboard shows:

- **Quick Start buttons** for immediate practice
- **Your progress** across all knowledge domains
- **Recent sessions** with scores and areas to improve
- **Domain mastery** showing your strongest and weakest areas

## Training Modes

### 1. Quick Practice

**Best for:** Warming up, casual practice, building general knowledge

**How it works:**
1. Tap **Quick Practice** on the dashboard
2. Choose question count (5, 10, 20, or custom)
3. Answer questions at your own pace
4. See instant feedback on each answer

**Tips:**
- Use voice or typing to answer
- Tap "Skip" if you're stuck
- Review explanations after each question

### 2. Written Round Training

**Best for:** Preparing for written/team rounds where you answer independently

**How it works:**
1. Tap **Written Round** from Training Modes
2. You get a set of questions (typically 40-60)
3. Answer all questions within the time limit
4. Submit when ready or when time expires

**Regional differences:**
- **Colorado:** 8 rounds of 8 questions each
- **Minnesota:** 4 rounds of 10 questions each
- **Washington:** 6 rounds of 10 questions each

**Tips:**
- Don't spend too long on hard questions
- Return to skipped questions if time permits
- Practice pacing yourself

### 3. Oral Round Training

**Best for:** Practicing buzzer strategy and verbal answers

**How it works:**
1. Tap **Oral Round** from Training Modes
2. Questions are read aloud (or displayed with timer)
3. Tap the buzzer when you know the answer
4. Speak your answer clearly

**Key skills:**
- Knowing when to buzz early vs. wait
- Clear articulation under pressure
- Confidence in your answers

**Regional conferring rules:**
- **Colorado:** No conferring allowed (individual answers only)
- **Minnesota:** 30 seconds team conference allowed
- **Washington:** 20 seconds team conference allowed

### 4. Domain Drill

**Best for:** Strengthening weak areas or deepening expertise

**How it works:**
1. Tap **Domain Drill** from Training Modes
2. Select a knowledge domain:
   - Science
   - Mathematics
   - Social Studies
   - Language Arts
   - Fine Arts
   - Current Events
3. Answer 10-20 focused questions
4. See your domain mastery improve

**Tips:**
- Start with your weakest domains
- Revisit domains regularly to maintain mastery
- Track your progress in the Analytics section

### 5. Conference Training (Colorado Springs Rules)

**Best for:** Teams practicing collaborative decision-making

**How it works:**
1. Tap **Conference Training** from Training Modes
2. Questions appear with timer
3. Practice signaling when you know the answer
4. Practice waiting for team consensus

**Features:**
- Hand signal tracking (for in-person team practice)
- Conference efficiency metrics
- Difficulty progression based on performance

### 6. Rebound Training

**Best for:** Practicing the critical "rebound" strategy

**What is a rebound?** When another team answers incorrectly, your team gets a chance to answer. Rebounds are crucial for winning matches!

**How it works:**
1. Tap **Rebound Training** from Training Modes
2. See a question and an opponent's wrong answer
3. Decide: Do you know the right answer?
4. If yes, answer. If unsure, pass.

**Skills developed:**
- Quick analysis of wrong answers
- Confidence calibration
- Risk assessment

**Metrics tracked:**
- Rebound accuracy (correct rebound answers)
- Rebound efficiency (rebounds attempted vs. available)
- Strategic passes (wisely avoiding wrong rebounds)

### 7. Match Simulation

**Best for:** Full competition experience with realistic pressure

**How it works:**
1. Tap **Match Simulation** from Training Modes
2. Choose competition level (Regional, State, National)
3. Play against 2 AI opponent teams
4. Complete all rounds (written + oral)
5. See final standings and detailed stats

**Realism features:**
- Timed rounds matching real competitions
- AI opponents with varying skill levels
- Regional rule enforcement
- Authentic scoring

**After match analysis:**
- Question-by-question breakdown
- Domain performance comparison
- Improvement recommendations

## Tips for Competition Success

### General Strategy

1. **Know your strengths:** Focus on contributing in your best domains
2. **Practice weak areas:** Don't avoid domains you struggle with
3. **Build speed:** Accurate answers mean nothing if you're too slow
4. **Learn from mistakes:** Review every wrong answer

### Written Round Strategy

1. Answer easy questions first
2. Don't leave questions blank (no penalty for guessing)
3. Use process of elimination
4. Budget time evenly across questions

### Oral Round Strategy

1. Buzz only when confident
2. Listen to the full question when unsure
3. Speak clearly and project your voice
4. Learn from opponent's wrong answers (rebounds!)

### Team Strategy

1. Assign domain specialists
2. Develop hand signals for communication
3. Practice conferring efficiently
4. Trust your teammates

## Regional Quick Reference

### Colorado

- **Teams per match:** 3
- **Team size:** 4 players
- **Oral conferring:** Not allowed (most competitions)
- **Written rounds:** 8 rounds of 8 questions
- **Scoring:** 5 points per oral, 1 point per written

### Minnesota

- **Teams per match:** 3
- **Team size:** 4-6 players (4 active in oral)
- **Oral conferring:** 30 seconds allowed
- **Written rounds:** 4 rounds of 10 questions
- **Scoring:** 5 points per oral, 1 point per written

### Washington

- **Teams per match:** 3
- **Team size:** 3-5 players (4 active in oral)
- **Oral conferring:** 20 seconds allowed
- **Written rounds:** 6 rounds of 10 questions
- **Scoring:** Variable by competition level

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many questions should I practice daily?**
A: We recommend at least 20-30 questions daily for serious competitors, but even 10 questions a day helps maintain knowledge.

**Q: Can I practice with my team?**
A: Yes! Conference Training mode is designed for team practice. Have your team gather around one device and practice signaling and conferring.

**Q: How does the AI opponent difficulty work?**
A: AI opponents adjust based on your performance. Start with "Regional" level and work up to "National" as you improve.

**Q: Are these real competition questions?**
A: Questions are designed to match competition difficulty and style, covering the same domains and topics as real Knowledge Bowl.

**Q: Can I import questions from my coach?**
A: Yes, coaches can import custom question sets. See your coach for details.

**Q: How does voice recognition work?**
A: UnaMentis uses advanced speech-to-text and answer validation to understand your spoken answers, even with slight pronunciation differences.

**Q: What if I disagree with an answer?**
A: Tap the "Report" button on any question to flag it for review. Include your reasoning and an alternative answer if applicable.
